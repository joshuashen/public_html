Open position: Postdoctoral Research Scientist

The Shen Lab in Department of Systems Biology at Columbia University is seeking a talented postdoctoral research scientist to develop new computational methods to study clonal dynamics of the immune system. The postdoc will be jointly mentored by Dr. Megan Sykes at The Columbia Center for Translational Immunology. The Sykes and Shen labs have ongoing collaboration to study the mechanism of immune tolerance in organ transplantation using high-throughput genomics and computational approaches.

Projects

The postdoc will lead the analysis in multi-disciplinary collaboration on genomic and translational immunology. Additionally, postdocs are encouraged to come up with new ideas in the field of computational and systems immunology. Here are a few examples:

  • Develop and apply new computational methods to model T cell receptor (TCR) repertoire in health subjects or patients with a range of conditions, such as organ transplantation and autoimmune diseases.
  • Develop new methods to predict specificity and strength of antigen recognition of TCR sequences.

Requirements

  • PhD in Computational Biology, Immunology, or equivalent
  • Demonstrated experience in high-throughput sequencing data analysis or machine learning
  • Excellent communication skills and teamwork
  • Programming: Fluency with at least one general-purpose programming language (such as Python, Swift), and one language/framework for scientific computing (such as R, TensorFlow). Experience with source code version control systems
  • Creative thinker and strong problem-solving skills

The salary of the position will be competitive based on qualifications.

Applicants should send a CV, a statement of interest, and contact information for three references to Yufeng.
